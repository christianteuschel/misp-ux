# Introduction

Hackathon.lu 2026, held in Luxembourg on 14–15 April 2026, is an established hackathon focused on open cybersecurity, organized by the team at CIRCL (https://www.circl.lu).

We used the hackathon as an opportunity to interview MISP users — to better understand how the platform is used today, align on user expectations, and distill use cases for AI-based extensions.

# Interview

## Structure

The interview follows a funnel structure: it starts broad with context, narrows to specific usage, and closes with pain points and wishes. This keeps the conversation natural while covering the essentials.

Total time: ~7–8 minutes

## Questions

1. **What's your role, and how does threat intelligence fit into your daily work?**
   *Establishes context — analyst, incident responder, SOC operator, CISO, etc.*

2. **How did you first come to use MISP, and how long have you been using it?**
   *Adoption path — was it mandated, discovered organically, inherited?*

3. **Walk me through a typical scenario where you open MISP. What are you trying to accomplish?**
   *This is the key question. It gets at real use cases without leading the answer. Let them narrate freely, then probe. Possible follow-ups: "How often does that happen — daily, weekly?", "What do you do with the output, and where does it go next?"*

4. **Which features or parts of MISP do you rely on most?**
   *Events, attributes, feeds, correlation, taxonomies, galaxies, sharing groups, API, etc. This reveals which functionality actually matters to them.*

5. **Do you share or receive threat intelligence through MISP with other organizations? If so, how does that work in practice?**
   *Sharing is a core MISP differentiator, so it's important to understand whether and how they use it.*

6. **What's the most frustrating or time-consuming part of working with MISP?**
   *Open-ended; let them vent. This often reveals the most actionable insights.*

7. **Is there anything you work around or avoid doing in MISP because it's too difficult?**
   *Catches unmet needs the previous question might miss — things they've given up on.*

8. **If you could change or add one thing to improve your experience, what would it be?**
   *Forces prioritization — their single top wish.*

9. **Is there anything about how you use MISP that I haven't asked about but should know?**
   *Catch-all; sometimes surfaces the most interesting insight.*

## Interviewer Notes

A few things to keep in mind during the session:

- **Do:** Let silence work for you — count to three before jumping in after they finish. People often add their best insight after a pause. Use "tell me more about that" liberally.

- **Don't:** Ask leading questions like "Do you find the UI confusing?" Instead, keep it open: "How do you find working with the interface?"

- **Watch for:** Workarounds, switches to other tools, or manual steps — these are signals of unmet needs, even when they aren't framed as complaints.

- **Capture:** Not just what they say but how they say it — enthusiasm, hesitation, or frustration in their tone tells you what matters most.
